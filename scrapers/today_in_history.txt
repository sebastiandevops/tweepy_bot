1945, Indonesia's declaration of independence On this day in 1945, Sukarno declared Indonesia's independence from the Netherlands, and, after the Dutch transferred sovereignty four years later, he served as the country's first president (1949–67).
2008, At the Olympics in Beijing, American swimmer Michael Phelps helped his team win the 4 × 100-metre medley relay, becoming the first athlete to win eight medals at a single Games.
1978, Ben L. Abruzzo, Maxie Anderson, and Larry Newman completed the first transatlantic balloon flight, in Double Eagle II. 
1945, English author George Orwell published Animal Farm, an anti-utopian satire that became a classic.
1896, George Washington Carmack unearthed gold in Bonanza Creek, a tributary of the Klondike River now in Yukon, Canada, setting off a gold rush into the Klondike valley.
1807, The first serviceable steamboat—the Clermont, designed by American engineer Robert Fulton—embarked on its maiden voyage.
1590, John White returned from England to Roanoke Island, off the coast of present-day North Carolina, and a search the following day found no trace of the colony (now called the Lost Colony) that he had left there three years earlier.
1965, Indonesia withdrew from the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development and the International Monetary Fund.
